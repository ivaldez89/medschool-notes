🧩 Category 🟡 ILC 🔵 IDC 🎯 Therapeutic Implications
🩺 Example Clinical Presentation 👵 Postmeno, ER+ mass; subtle/asymmetric thickening (often underestimated on 🩻 mammogram); later GI/peritoneal spread 🫄 🤏 Palpable spiculated lump; clear imaging correlate; later 🦴 bone pain 🧭 ILC: order MRI for accurate extent, endocrine-first in many cases
📊 Prevalence ~10% of BCs Most common subtype 📝 ILC underrepresented in trials → don’t assume IDC data applies
🔬 Morphology “Single-file” 🧵 cells; hallmark = E-cadherin loss 🚫 Cohesive nests/glands 🧱 Loss of cohesion = diffuse margins, hard to excise completely
🧬 Genomics ↑ CDH1, PTEN loss/AKT ↑, TBX3, FOXA1; ↓ GATA3 🧬 More GATA3; rare CDH1 Explains differential endocrine sensitivity/resistance
🌸 Hormone/Proliferation Mostly ER+, HER2–; low Ki-67 🌿; Luminal A–like Mixed: Luminal A/B, HER2+, TNBC Endocrine-driven in ILC
🧭 Spread GI 🍽️, peritoneum 🫄, ovaries 🥚 Bone 🦴, lung 🌬️, liver 🧫 Tailor staging/surveillance → look beyond classic bone/liver mets
💉 Chemo Response Low pCR (~3–5%) ⚠️; less downstaging Higher pCR (~14%) ✅; more BCS possible Chemo sparingly in ILC; biomarker-driven approach
🧪 Oncotype DX Rarely high RS (~1.5%); many intermediate 🤔 Up to 20% high RS Intermediate less predictive in ILC → combine with pathology
🧿 HER2 & Trastuzumab Classic ILC 3–5%; mixed up to ~10% ➕; trastuzumab works 💉 15–20%; standard anti-HER2 therapy Don’t skip HER2 testing in ILC → benefit ≈ IDC
🌡️ Endocrine Therapy AIs (letrozole) better than TAM ➕🟢 AI benefit clearest in Luminal B Postmeno ILC: AI first-line
📉 Prognosis Slower growing ⏳ but risk of late recurrence; atypical mets patterns More chemo-sensitive, early downstaging 💥 ILC: need MRI, wide margins, watch late relapse & GI mets
